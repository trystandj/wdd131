Guides to Taking Better Shots

Improve Your FPV Journey

Plan Your Shots Ahead

Scout Locations: Whether it’s for a race or a scenic film, knowing the area before flying helps. Look for interesting visual cues like tall buildings, trees, or winding rivers to make your shots more engaging.

Bird's Eye View

Adjust your camera tilt (0°-15°) for better top-down angles, use smooth throttle control to maintain stability, and experiment with flight techniques like hover spins and slow descents. Optimize camera settings (4K, 30-60fps, ND filters) and avoid common mistakes like over-tilting or losing altitude. With practice, you can take your FPV footage to the next level!

Smooth Flying Movements

Master Smooth Flying Movements: Achieve cinematic FPV footage with controlled and fluid motions. Smooth flight paths enhance tracking shots, dives, and low-to-the-ground maneuvers. With patience and practice, your FPV flights will look seamless and professional!
